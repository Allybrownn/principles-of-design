Balance

From the textbook White Space is Not Your Enemy: in design, we think of balance in terms of visual weight. You want your designs and layouts to be visually balanced, unless your communication purpose is to unsettle people by making them feel unbalanced and tense or anxious. There are three kinds of visual balance: radial, symmetrical and asymmetrical.

Balance is the element of design that allows viewers to see the design as a sensible and pleasing design. Essentially to create balance in a design there should be an arrangement of objects to create a feeling of equal weight, often created through use of symmetry.
